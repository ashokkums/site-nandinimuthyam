Arushya

A school rooted in the Kalakshetra tradition — where classical form meets living practice, and every student finds their own beginning.

About the school

Arushya School of Dance was founded by Nandini Muthyam in 2003 in Chennai, with a single conviction: that classical Bharatanatyam should be accessible to anyone drawn to it — child or adult, beginner or returning student. The school is now based in the Austin, Texas area, continuing that same mission from its new home.

Arushya teaches in the world-renowned Kalakshetra style of Bharatanatyam — a tradition distinguished by its precision of line, depth of abhinaya, and fidelity to classical Carnatic musical structure. The curriculum is structured progressively, guiding students from foundational adavus through to full-length margam repertoire.

Founder & Principal

Nandini trained under stalwarts of the form — Shri Adayar K. Lakshman and Smt. Kalanidhi Narayanan — absorbing not only the technical rigour of the Kalakshetra school but its deeper philosophy: that technique is only the beginning, and that dance, at its fullest, is a conversation between the body and something larger than itself.

With over a decade of dedicated teaching experience, she brings to the studio the same quality of attention she brings to the stage — patient, exacting, and genuinely invested in each student's arc. Her Margazhi Festival arangetram in December 2024 marked a return to the concert stage, affirming both her continuing practice and her commitment to keeping the form alive through performance.

Classes

Arushya offers structured Bharatanatyam instruction for students of all levels, from young beginners to adults returning to dance. Classes follow the Kalakshetra syllabus and are taught in small cohorts to ensure individual attention. The school welcomes students across the Austin and Round Rock area.

Lineage

Trained under Shri Adayar K. Lakshman — one of the foremost exponents of the Kalakshetra style — and the legendary Smt. Kalanidhi Narayanan, whose mastery of abhinaya (expressive mime) shaped a generation of classical artists. That inheritance informs every class at Arushya: the exactness of the body, the intelligence of the eye, and the commitment to emotional truth.
